# EMI Policy Analysis

## Overview

Vietnam's EMI (English as a Medium of Instruction) initiative represents a 20-year national transformation plan to make English the second language in all schools by 2045. This document analyzes the key policy instruments and their implications for Learneris.

## Key Policy Instruments

### 1. QD 2371/QD-TTg — The National Blueprint

**Issued**: October 27, 2025 by the Prime Minister
**Title**: "Making English the Second Language in Schools 2025-2035, Vision to 2045"

This is the apex policy document. It establishes:
- A 3-level framework for measuring EMI readiness/implementation at schools
- National targets by education level (preschool, K-12, vocational, university)
- The mandate for provinces/cities to create their own implementation plans
- Authorization for public-private partnerships in delivery

### 2. NQ 71-NQ/TW — Politburo Education Breakthrough

**Issued**: August 22, 2025 by the Politburo
**Title**: "Breakthrough Development of Education and Training"

This resolution provides the top-level political backing for:
- Fundamental reform of education methodology
- Integration of AI and technology in teaching
- International integration as a core education goal

### 3. HCMC De An — The City Implementation Plan

**Status**: Draft (2026), pending UBND HCMC approval
**Scope**: All education levels in HCMC (now including merged Binh Duong and Ba Ria - Vung Tau)

Key specifics:
- **Phase 1 (2025-2030)**: Foundation and standardization
  - 70% preschools ready for English integration
  - 100% K-12 teaching English from Grade 1
  - 30% schools at Level 1, 20% at Level 2, 10% at Level 3
- **Phase 2 (2030-2040)**: Expansion and ecosystem formation
- **Phase 3 (2040-2045)**: Complete integration

#### The 4-Step Implementation Model
1. Improve teaching quality and standardize programs
2. Expand English usage environment beyond the classroom
3. **Apply technology and digital transformation — develop online learning platforms, bilingual digital content, integrate AI**
4. Pilot and scale

#### Explicit Technology Mandates
- "Develop online learning platforms, bilingual digital content repositories"
- "Integrate AI into supporting English learning"
- The shift from EFL (English as Foreign Language) to ESL (English as Second Language) model
- CLIL (Content and Language Integrated Learning) as the core methodology

### 4. SGDDT-GDMN 2551 — Preschool Implementation

**Issued**: 2026 by HCMC Department of Education
**Deadline**: Schools must register for EMI pilot by May 14, 2026

This directive implements QD 2371 at the preschool level:
- Encourages all preschools with sufficient conditions to pilot
- 1-2 classes per age group as minimum pilot scope
- Monitoring and reporting built in
- Phase: starting AY 2026-2027

### 5. KH 18/KH-BGDDT — Teacher Proficiency Survey

**Issued**: 2026 by Ministry of Education
**Timeline**: January-June 2026

A national teacher English proficiency survey covering:
- Survey planning (Jan 2026)
- School selection (Feb 2026)
- Tool preparation (Feb 2026)
- Survey execution (Apr 2026)
- Results analysis (May 2026)
- Report (Jun 2026)

## Policy Gaps = Product Opportunities

| Gap in Policy | Opportunity for Learneris |
|---------------|--------------------------|
| No tool for schools to self-assess EMI readiness | EMI Readiness Assessment Platform |
| No bilingual content repository exists at scale | Bilingual Content Engine (CLIL) |
| Teachers lack real-time EMI support | EMI Teacher Companion App |
| No monitoring/reporting tool for DoE | EMI School Dashboard |
| No CLIL training pathway | Extension of PD-Slides for CLIL |
| No student practice environment for EMI | Student English Immersion Environment |
| Teacher proficiency survey requires tools | Assessment platform integration |

## Regulatory Risk Factors

1. **Platform role limitation**: Documents carefully state technology "does not replace teachers" and "does not participate in education management" — Learneris must maintain this positioning
2. **No curriculum changes**: EMI must work within existing GDPT 2018 curriculum
3. **No teacher proficiency assessment**: The policy explicitly states teacher English proficiency is NOT evaluated (politically sensitive)
4. **Public school budget constraints**: Especially in newly-merged outer districts; socialization (xa hoi hoa) funding model required
