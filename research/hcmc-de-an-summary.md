# HCMC De An Summary

## Document: "Making English the Second Language in Schools in HCMC 2025-2035, Vision to 2045"

**Status**: Draft, pending UBND HCMC approval (2026)
**Scope**: All education levels — preschool, K-12, vocational, university, continuing education
**Geographic scope**: HCMC (post-merger with Binh Duong and Ba Ria-Vung Tau)

## Targets by Level and Phase

### Preschool (Mam Non)
| Phase | Target |
|-------|--------|
| 2025-2030 | 70% institutions ready; 20% at Level 1 |
| 2030-2035 | 80% ready; 30% Level 1; 10% Level 2 |
| 2040 | 90% ready; 40% Level 1; 15% Level 2; 5% Level 3 |
| 2045 | 100% ready; 50% Level 1; 20% Level 2; 10% Level 3 |

### K-12 (Pho Thong)
| Phase | Target |
|-------|--------|
| 2025-2030 | 100% teaching English from Grade 1; 30% Level 1; 20% Level 2; 10% Level 3 |
| 2030-2035 | 50% Level 1; 30% Level 2; 20% Level 3 |

### University (Dai Hoc)
| Phase | Target |
|-------|--------|
| 2025-2030 | 20% Level 1; 5% Level 2; 3% Level 3 |
| 2030-2035 | 30% Level 1; 20% Level 2; 5% Level 3 |

### Vocational (Nghe Nghiep)
| Phase | Target |
|-------|--------|
| 2025-2030 | 15% Level 1; 5% Level 2 |

## Current State Data (HCMC)

- **50,300** public school teachers surveyed for English proficiency
- **80%+** of English teachers at primary/THCS have B2+ proficiency
- **50%+** of THPT English teachers at C1/C2
- **29.79%** of vocational teachers at B1+
- **1,235/1,832** (67.4%) preschools already running English familiarity programs
- **206,811/387,556** (53.4%) preschool children participating in English programs
- **90%+** public schools have intensive English or bilingual programs
- **8 consecutive years** HCMC leads the nation in English exam scores
- **1,915** English language centers operating in HCMC

## Key Challenges Identified

1. **Teacher capacity**: Most non-English teachers below B1; huge gap between English specialists and subject teachers
2. **Urban-suburban disparity**: Post-merger districts lack infrastructure and technology
3. **Budget pressure**: Scaling across newly merged areas strains local budgets
4. **Parent awareness**: Outer districts have lower parent buy-in for English emphasis

## Technology Mandates

The De An explicitly identifies as Step 3:
> "Apply technology and digital transformation — develop online learning platforms, bilingual digital content repositories. Integrate AI into supporting English learning."

AI specifically mentioned alongside ELSA Speak and Grammarly as examples of tools that can:
- Personalize learning pathways
- Provide instant pronunciation feedback
- Create interactive practice environments via chatbots
- Help teachers prepare lessons and create exercises
